  3. Britannica Chlorine is the second-lightest member of the halogen group of elements. It is a greenish-yellow gas that is heavier than air and has a pungent, irritating odor. Chlorine is a powerful oxidizing agent and is very reactive. It is not found free in nature, but it is a major component of many common minerals, including sodium chloride (table salt) and potassium chloride. Chlorine is used in a wide variety of industrial processes, including the production of paper, plastics, and textiles. It is also used as a disinfectant for drinking water and swimming pools.
