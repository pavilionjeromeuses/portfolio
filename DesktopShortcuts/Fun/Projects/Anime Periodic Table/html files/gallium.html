  1. Royal Society of Chemistry [RSC] Gallium is a soft, silvery metal that is similar in appearance to aluminum. It is a chemical element with the symbol Ga and atomic number 31. Gallium is found in trace amounts in bauxite, coal, and zinc ores. It is a useful element in the electronics industry, where it is used in semiconductors and light-emitting diodes (LEDs).

  2. Wikipedia Gallium is a chemical element with the symbol Ga and atomic number 31. Discovered by French chemist Paul-Émile Lecoq de Boisbaudran in 1875, gallium is a soft, silvery-blue metal at room temperature. It is notable for its low melting point (29.76 °C or 85.57 °F), which is just above room temperature.
    ガリウムは、記号 Ga および原子番号 31 の化学元素です。1875 年にフランスの化学者ポール エミール ルコック ド ボアボードランによって発見されたガリウムは、室温では柔らかい銀青色の金属です。室温よりわずかに高い融点 (29.76 °C または 85.57 °F) が低いことで注目に値します。     [ ʕ•ᴥ•ʔ ]
